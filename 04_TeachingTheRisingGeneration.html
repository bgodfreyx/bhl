01 Introduction

Deeper Dive: Teaching the Rising Generation

This first section is about teaching children and youth. As young people learn about Jesus Christ and try to follow His example, they too can help build Zion. From its early days, the Church has supported the care and teaching of each rising generation.

Here at this site, Brigham Young’s daughters formed what would become the Church’s worldwide organization for young women, Eliza R. Snow championed the formation of an organization for young children in the Church called Primary, and Joseph F. Smith instituted the Church’s family home evening program during his presidency. Over the years, Church leaders have adjusted programs to better meet the needs and circumstances of young people around the world as they strive to follow the Savior.

Look in the artifact case. Do you notice the hand-colored book? This handbook belonged to a young German girl in the 1930s named Helga Meiszus. Helga recorded her achievements in fields such as health, nature, and public service. Being a member of the Beehive program, the Church’s first achievement program for young women, helped her develop as a daughter of God in ways that other youth programs at the time could not.

Teaching children about Jesus Christ can be done by people of any age. On the opposite wall, you will see a picture of a young girl. That’s Delia Rochon. At thirteen years old, and just months after she joined the Church, she led the children’s organization in her local congregation in Uruguay. This role is traditionally done by an adult. Delia accepted the tremendous responsibility and trusted the Lord to guide her in teaching the younger children. In addition to blessing the younger children, this role helped Delia deepen her own faith. As she remembered, “It was the best time of my life because I was learning the gospel at the same time that I was teaching.”
