Deeper Dive: Naamah Carter Twiss Young

Deeper Dive: Naamah Carter Twiss Young

Take a moment to look at the full-length photograph on the wall. It shows Naamah Carter Twiss Young—affectionately known to the Young family as “Aunt Twiss.”

Naamah joined the Church in New Hampshire in 1842. Three years later, she moved to Illinois and married John Sandersen Twiss. They were only married a few months before John died. On his deathbed, he asked Brigham Young to take care of his beloved Naamah. Before the Latter-day Saints left Illinois in early 1846, Brigham and Naamah were sealed in the Nauvoo Temple. Years later, toward the end of Brigham’s life, Brigham travelled with Naamah to the newly completed St. George temple. There, Brigham stood as proxy for her first husband, John, so she could be sealed to him for eternity.

Family members remembered Aunt Twiss as kind, tactful, and generous. Though she never had children of her own, the children in the Young family and others saw her as a second mother. The orphaned Joseph F. Smith later recalled Aunt Twiss’s kindness toward him and how “her chief aim in life” seemed to be “mak[ing] everybody happy.”

Here in the Lion House, her influence was felt every day. Skilled in cooking and home management, Aunt Twiss oversaw the kitchen and helped maintain the rhythm and order of the household for decades. After living in this home for 44 years, she moved in with one of Brigham’s children for the final nine years of her life. She was the last member of Brigham Young’s family to move out of the Lion House.
