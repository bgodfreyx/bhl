Deeper Dive: Joseph F. Smith and Prophetic Revelation

After Brigham Young and Lorenzo Snow, Joseph F. Smith became the third President of the Church to live in the Beehive House. He lived here with one of his five plural wives, Julina, and their younger children.

As a residence for prophets, the Beehive House became a place of revelation from God. Here, revelation was sought, received, shared, and implemented. In October 1918, while sitting in his room in the Beehive House, Joseph F. Smith received a divine vision from the Lord about the afterlife, which would come to be known as the vision of the redemption of the dead. This revelation was later canonized as section 138 of the Doctrine and Covenants and has provided guidance and encouragement for Latter-day Saints to extend the blessings of the gospel of Jesus Christ and eternal families to those who have died.
