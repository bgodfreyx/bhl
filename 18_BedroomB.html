Bedroom B

Bedroom B

Not all the wives’ rooms have been restored, so in this space you will hear stories about multiple wives. Some wives had rooms with alcoves, like this one, where their little children could sleep.

Lucy Ann Decker Young lived in this room with her younger children when the Lion House was first completed. The room is set up as if Lucy Ann slept in one alcove and her children slept in the other.

Later, she and her children moved to the Beehive House, where Lucy Ann served as matron for more than two decades. Her leadership in both homes shaped a family culture of service.

Lucy Ann’s sister Clara also married Brigham and lived in a bedroom similar to this one with her young children. One of Brigham’s daughters remembered that Clara’s fire always burned bright. If any of the daughters in the family needed a sympathetic listening ear, they visited Clara. Picture the young teenage girls quietly sitting on the floor in the dark of the evening to talk with Clara by the light and warmth of the fire.
