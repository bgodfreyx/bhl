Deeper Dive: Tour of the Holy Land

Deeper Dive: Tour of the Holy Land

Take a look at the bookshelf in the room, where you’ll find a few objects on display behind glass. In the latter part of the 19th century, it was a common practice for Americans to take a grand tour of Europe and the Holy Land. From 1872 to 1873, Eliza accompanied a group of Latter-day Saint leaders on a trip through Europe, Palestine, and Egypt. She brought back mementos of her trip, including shells, salt from the Dead Sea, and a photo taken in Egypt—all of which are displayed in the bookcase. Throughout the trip, a newspaper in Utah published letters and poems sent back from Eliza and other members of the group to allow Latter-day Saints to vicariously participate in the journey.
