Deeper Dive: The Poetess

Eliza was well known in her lifetime as a poetess. Her first poem was published when she was 21 years old in Ohio, and the last to be published appeared a few weeks before her death at age 83. Many poems are found in her journals and personal papers. Several of Eliza’s poems have been set to music and have become beloved Latter-day Saint hymns. Other poems were deeply cherished by the friends and loved ones for whom she wrote.

One example is a poem she composed for Brigham’s wife Margaret Pierce Young. During the journey across the plains, Eliza rode with Margaret and Margaret’s parents in their nicely sprung buggy, making the journey much more comfortable for Eliza than other accommodations would have been. The two women became close friends, and both eventually had rooms on the top floor of the Lion House. To Margaret, Eliza wrote:

I love thee with a tenderness as sister spirits love.

I love thee for thy loveliness is like to theirs above.

I love thee for thy modest worth is like a diadem;

Thou surely are of noble birth, thou are a precious gem.

Margaret cherished these lines and noted them in her autobiography more than 50 years later.
