Bedroom E

Bedroom E

The top floor of the Lion House once had 20 bedrooms. Brigham’s sons Willard and Ernest shared one that may have looked similar to this one. On the wall, you can see clothing hanging up. One set represents a workman’s clothes. As teenagers, Willard and Ernest asked if they could quit school and go to work. Brigham gave his permission for them to work for nine months and go to school for three during the winter.

Willard later excelled as a student. He became the first Latter-day Saint appointed to the Army’s military college, West Point, in 1871. The other clothing on the wall is an army cadet’s uniform.

You are welcome to dress up as a workman or an army cadet. Please hang the clothes up before you go.
